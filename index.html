Save the Nature

Since the dawn of time, humans have relied heavily on nature for resources to thrive and continue to grow as a society and as a nation. But as of the last few hundred years, humans have started to abuse nature and ruin the balance that has been upheld for thousands of years. This took a sharp rise at the start of the industrial revolution due to the fumes being released. After the revolution, the world saw a large increase in population and an increase in living standards, results in the depletion of resources. The usage of different types of fuels and chemicals in factories resulted in increased air and water pollution along with an increase in the usage of fossil fuels. Then the 20th century rolls along and brings with it many inventions. The inventions weren’t exactly energy efficient, but they worked. It also brought along plenty of ways to minimize fuel emissions. As time went on, people decided to find new ways to optimize inventions and minimize emissions. It’s not perfect just yet, but we’ll get there soon hopefully. Of course, you can also do your part, by managing electricity and heating in your home and workplace. You can also make sure to use public transport to minimize the emissions of fossil fuels. Also, by taking shorter showers, you can lower the burning of fossil fuels.
